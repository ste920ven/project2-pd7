What You Need To Know

  

Well, this is it.

It's December 21 2012, and the world is ending.

            So, naturally of course, you've come here to figure out how to survive the apocalypse. That's a good start, but you still are not sure exactly what we can do for you. It's simple. No matter what sort of world-ending destruction is threatening your existence, we've figured out just how feasible it is for you to wait out the hurricane/fire/alien attack/zombie plague no matter where you are. Simply enter your location and we'll tell you some nearby places that you should either stay near or avoid at all costs!         

            For example, lets say you and your buddies are holed up in the Statue of Liberty during an alien invasion. Wait, seriously? COME ON, haven't you seen "Independence Day"??? Why would you even be there in the first place??? Well, as it turns out large landmarks are a bad place to be during an alien invasion (something to do with symbolically destroying humanity).

   

In any case, we wish you well and hope that you survive the cataclysmic destruction of humanity! 

 

Some Useful Disaster Tips

   

1. On The Hurricane 

            A Hurricane is a large storm comprised of clouds, thunder, lightning, wind, and rain. It typically has an eye, but it is blind. Calling yourself 'Nobody' will not save you from a Hurricane however, as they tend to disregard this sort of thing. Hurricanes are born when the spirits of angry drowned marching-band brass players get together and decide to ruin everyone else's parade. Typically, Apocalyptic Hurricanes occur when the spirits of rival marching bands run into each other and try to outdoo the other's Hurricane which really just results in one big mess much to the distress of those still living.

 

To save yourself:

            1. DON'T GO OUTSIDE. Seriously, you might get knocked off by all manner of objects caught in the wind: branches, bricks, or even turkeys. And turkeys are not a nice way to go.

            2. Stock up on foodstuffs. In the days after the hurricane has died down, you can use the food as leverage to get valuable commodities such as year-long subscriptions to WoW.

            3. Keep cleaning supplies handy. You never know when the Johnsons might visit.

            4. In case your house/hovel/cardboard box is destroyed by the Hurricane, immediately find another shelter. No, umbrellas do not count. Unless Rihanna is holding one, in which case you're probably inside a mansion somewhere anyways.

 

2. On Fire

OW OW OW OW OWEEEEE OW OW OW OWEEE OWOWOWWOWEEEE EOWOWOOOOOOW WEEEEWOEO WEWO OWOWOW OWWWWWWWWCHEEEEEE    EEEEEEEEEEEE

 

If you are on Fire:

             Extinguish the fire through calm and controlled means. While screaming at the top of your lungs can be a good idea (in case you need help or the fire is capable of being scared away) it typically however only exacerbates the situation.

           

If there is a Fire around you:

            Endeavor to extinguish the fire. Contact the fire department. Try to reason with it, and see if it can be persuaded to let itself burn out.

If the Fire cannot be controlled (ex: Daemon hellfire or that stuff from the last Harry Potter book) alert others to the Fire's presence and try to get everyone away from the Fire in a safe and orderly manner as quickly as possible. Do not run back to save the cat.

 

 

3. On The Zombie

            The Zombie is an animated corpse. It is a member of the elitist 'Undead' club, along with the vampires (sparkly and non-sparkly) and average DMV workers. The Zombie enjoys eating human brains, which is why it often goes to great lengths to get its favorite treat. If a Zombie bites a living human being, than that human being first loses fine motor controls, then the ability to play mahjong, and finally the capacity to distinguish between country rock and heavy metal, at which point the transformation is complete. A Zombie Apocalypse involves lots of Zombies getting together and ridding the world of Beliebers. Unfortunately, Zombies can’t really tell the difference between Beliebers and normal humans.

 

If you face a horde of Zombies who think you are a Belieber:

            1. Don’t start singing that song by Neil Diamond, ‘Believer’ and “’Belieber’ sound waaaaay too similar.

            2. Get yourself some weapons to fight the Zombies off. They’re scared of Fire (See ‘On Fire’) and simple magic tricks.

            3. If you find yourself surrounded with no where to run than you should play Dead. The Grateful Dead, that is; preferably on a massive boom box with tricked-out subwoofers.

 

4. On The Aliens

            An Alien is simply any denizen of the universe that isn’t native to planet Earth. Aliens come in many shapes and sizes, but they all have a deeply profound (and totally not personal, they promise) need to extinguish the human race, born from years of deep, calculated and logical though which cumulated in the decision that another hyperspace bypass was in order (which of course requires that the planet remain intact, but that all life forms on it be destroyed).

 

In case all native life forms on Earth face extinction:

1. Grab your towel.

2. Grab your copy of the Guide.

3. Hitch a ride on the nearest spaceship (yes, even if they are Vogons).

4. Identify what type of Alien you now must deal with. For your convenience, we have amassed short descriptions of some lesser-known species.

 

The Decepticons

 

Decepticons are gigantic robots composed of vehicle parts, and they really would like nothing more than to see your feeble organic body smushed to pulp. Led by Megatron, they can transform into various mechanical contraptions, such as cars, planes, and old-timey watches.

 

Kang and Kodos

 

These two slime-green beasties hail from Rigel IV, and have bided their time for years trying to understand human culture. However, one brave man realized what they were up to, and decided to devote his life to acting in an often dumb fashion in order to trick them into believing humankind was weak, so that when they did eventually strike they could easily be thwarted, unprepared as they would be.
